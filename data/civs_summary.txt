# Civilization Summary Data
# Format: === CIV_NAME === followed by content
# Used by the AI Advisor to understand civilization-specific units, infrastructure, and biases

=== AMERICA ===
Unique Unit: P-51 Mustang (Modern Era fighter with extended range and bonus vs fighters)
Unique Unit: Rough Rider (Industrial Era cavalry, gains culture from kills on home continent)
Unique Building: Film Studio (Broadcast Center replacement, +100% tourism pressure to other civs in Modern Era)
Bias: Culture/Diplomacy. Strong late-game tourism. Founding Father ability grants legacy bonuses.

=== SUMERIA ===
Unique Unit: War Cart (Ancient Era heavy cavalry, no penalty vs anti-cavalry, doesn't require horses)
Unique Improvement: Ziggurat (+2 Science, +1 Culture if adjacent to river)
Bias: Early Domination/Science. War Carts are extremely powerful early rush units. Ziggurats provide strong early science.

=== ROME ===
Unique Unit: Legion (Classical Era swordsman, can build forts and roads)
Unique Building: Baths (Aqueduct replacement, +2 Housing, +1 Amenity)
Bias: Expansion/Domination. All cities start with a Trading Post. Free roads to capital within trade route range.

=== GREECE ===
Unique Unit: Hoplite (Ancient Era anti-cavalry, +10 combat when adjacent to other Hoplites)
Unique District: Acropolis (Theater Square replacement, must be built on hills, +1 Culture from adjacent districts)
Bias: Culture/Diplomacy. One extra Wildcard policy slot. Strong early defense with Hoplites.

=== EGYPT ===
Unique Unit: Maryannu Chariot Archer (Ancient Era ranged cavalry)
Unique Improvement: Sphinx (+1 Faith, +1 Culture, +2 Appeal, bonus adjacent to Wonders)
Bias: Wonder Building. +15% Production toward Wonders. Floodplains don't block districts.

=== CHINA ===
Unique Unit: Crouching Tiger (Medieval Era ranged unit, no resource requirement)
Unique Improvement: Great Wall (provides Gold, Culture, and Tourism, must be built along borders)
Bias: Wonder/Culture. Eurekas and Inspirations grant 60% progress instead of 50%.

=== BABYLON ===
Unique Unit: Sabum Kibittum (Ancient Era melee, +17 combat vs heavy/light cavalry)
Unique Building: Palgum (Water Mill replacement, +2 Food, +1 Housing, freshwater bonus to farms)
Bias: Science Victory. Eurekas grant full tech completion. Reduced base science per turn to compensate.

=== JAPAN ===
Unique Unit: Samurai (Medieval Era melee, no combat penalty when damaged)
Unique Building: Electronics Factory (Factory replacement, +4 Production, +4 Culture to all cities within 6 tiles)
Bias: Flexible/Domination. Districts receive +1 adjacency from other districts. Half-cost Encampment, Holy Site, Theater.

=== ENGLAND ===
Unique Unit: Sea Dog (Renaissance Era naval raider, can capture enemy ships)
Unique Unit: Redcoat (Industrial Era melee, +10 combat on foreign continent)
Unique District: Royal Navy Dockyard (Harbor replacement, +1 Movement for naval units, +2 Gold per Dockyard elsewhere)
Bias: Naval/Expansion. Extra Archaeological Museum slots. Strong colonial gameplay.

=== FRANCE ===
Unique Unit: Garde Imperiale (Industrial Era melee, +10 combat near capital, Great General bonus)
Unique Improvement: Chateau (+2 Culture, +1 Gold, must be adjacent to river)
Bias: Culture/Tourism. +20% Production toward Medieval+ Wonders. Double tourism from Wonders.

=== GERMANY ===
Unique Unit: U-Boat (Modern Era submarine, cheaper, +10 combat in ocean, can see other subs)
Unique District: Hansa (Industrial Zone replacement, +2 Production from adjacent Commercial Hub, resources)
Bias: Production/Domination. +1 Military policy slot. Cities can build one extra district beyond population limit.

=== RUSSIA ===
Unique Unit: Cossack (Industrial Era cavalry, +5 combat in/near home territory, can move after attacking)
Unique District: Lavra (Holy Site replacement, +1 Great Prophet/Writer/Artist/Musician point per turn)
Bias: Religion/Expansion. Extra territory when founding cities. +1 Faith and +1 Production from Tundra.

=== BRAZIL ===
Unique Unit: Minas Geraes (Industrial Era battleship, improved defense)
Unique District: Street Carnival (Entertainment Complex replacement, cheaper)
Bias: Culture/Religion. Rainforests provide +1 adjacency to all districts. Great People points +20% during Golden Ages.

=== INDIA ===
Unique Unit: Varu (Classical Era heavy cavalry, adjacent enemies get -5 combat)
Unique Improvement: Stepwell (+1 Food, +1 Housing, Faith if adjacent to Holy Site)
Bias: Religion/Faith. +2 Spread Religion charges for Missionaries. +100% Religious pressure from Trade Routes.

=== ARABIA ===
Unique Unit: Mamluk (Medieval Era cavalry, heals at end of every turn)
Unique Building: Madrasa (University replacement, +5 Science, generates Faith equal to campus adjacency)
Bias: Religion/Science. Automatically gains last Great Prophet. +1 Science per foreign city following your religion.

=== KONGO ===
Unique Unit: Ngao Mbeba (Swordsman replacement, +10 vs ranged, no iron required)
Unique District: Mbanza (Neighborhood replacement, available earlier, +5 Housing, +2 Food, +4 Gold)
Bias: Culture/Relics. Cannot found religion but gains bonuses from others' religions. Double Great Writer/Artist/Musician points.

=== SPAIN ===
Unique Unit: Conquistador (Musketman replacement, +10 combat if Missionary/Apostle adjacent)
Unique Improvement: Mission (+2 Faith, +1 Science if on foreign continent)
Bias: Religion/Expansion. Trade Routes between continents grant bonus yields. Inquisitors can remove heresy.

=== AZTEC ===
Unique Unit: Eagle Warrior (Warrior replacement, can capture defeated units as Builders)
Unique Building: Tlachtli (Arena replacement, +2 Faith, +1 Great General point)
Bias: Domination/Production. Builders can speed district construction. Luxury resources provide +1 Amenity to additional cities.

=== SCYTHIA ===
Unique Unit: Saka Horse Archer (Classical Era ranged cavalry, cheap, fast)
Unique Improvement: Kurgan (+1 Faith, +1 Gold, +1 Faith for adjacent Pastures)
Bias: Domination/Cavalry. Build two light cavalry/Saka for the price of one. +5 combat vs wounded units.

=== NORWAY ===
Unique Unit: Berserker (Medieval Era melee, +10 attack, -5 defense, bonus movement after attacking)
Unique Unit: Longship (Ancient Era naval melee, can heal in neutral territory, coastal raids)
Unique Building: Stave Church (Temple replacement, +1 Faith for adjacent woods)
Bias: Naval/Domination. All naval melee units can coastal raid. Embarked units have ocean capability early.

=== NUBIA ===
Unique Unit: Pitati Archer (Ancient Era ranged, +1 Movement, replaces Archer)
Unique Improvement: Nubian Pyramid (+2 Faith, +2 Food/Production/Gold/Science depending on adjacent districts)
Bias: Ranged Units/Production. +50% Production toward ranged units. Mines over strategic resources provide +1 Production.

=== MACEDONIA ===
Unique Unit: Hypaspist (Swordsman replacement, +5 vs districts, +50% support bonus)
Unique Unit: Hetairoi (Horseman replacement, +5 Great General points from kills, free promotion)
Unique Building: Basilikoi Paides (Barracks replacement, +25% combat XP, science from kills)
Bias: Domination. Cities don't war weariness. Eurekas for killing units. Siege units siege without setup.

=== PERSIA ===
Unique Unit: Immortal (Swordsman replacement, +2 Movement, ranged attack capability)
Unique Improvement: Pairidaeza (+1 Culture, +2 Gold, +2 Appeal, +1 Culture per adjacent Holy Site/Theater)
Bias: Culture/Domination. +1 Movement during Golden Age. Surprise Wars have no penalty, declaring normal wars has no penalty in Golden Age.

=== AUSTRALIA ===
Unique Unit: Digger (Infantry replacement, +10 combat outside Australian territory, +5 adjacent to water)
Unique Improvement: Outback Station (+1 Food, +1 Production, +0.5 Housing, bonus for adjacent Pastures)
Bias: Flexible. +100% Production when war declared on Australia. +3 Housing in coastal cities. +1 Production from Pastures.

=== POLAND ===
Unique Unit: Winged Hussar (Cavalry replacement, pushes back defenders on attack if it does more damage)
Unique Building: Sukiennice (Market replacement, +4 Gold, +2 Production from international Trade Routes)
Bias: Religion/Domination. Military unit converts adjacent city when capturing with a Relic. Relics provide +4 Gold, +2 Faith, +2 Culture.

=== INDONESIA ===
Unique Unit: Jong (Frigate replacement, +5 combat, forms formations with naval)
Unique Improvement: Kampung (+1 Housing, +1 Production, +1 Food for adjacent Fishing Boats)
Bias: Naval/Faith. Coast tiles provide +0.5 Faith. Entertainment Complexes give +1 Amenity if adjacent to coast.

=== CREE ===
Unique Unit: Okihtcitaw (Scout replacement, free promotion, +5 combat strength)
Unique Improvement: Mekewap (+1 Housing, +2 Gold, +1 Production/Food for adjacent Bonus/Luxury)

=== GEORGIA ===
Unique Unit: Khevsur (Swordsman replacement, +7 combat in hills)

=== KOREA ===
Unique Unit: Hwacha (Field Cannon replacement, can't move and attack same turn, +10 combat)
Unique District: Seowon (Campus replacement, +4 Science, loses adjacency bonus from other districts)

=== MONGOLIA ===
Unique Unit: Keshig (Medieval Era ranged cavalry, other units in formation move at Keshig speed)
Unique Building: Ordu (Stable replacement, +1 Movement for cavalry class units)

=== NETHERLANDS ===

Unique Improvement: Polder (+1 Food, +1 Production, +0.5 Housing, +2 Gold, +1 Culture, +1 Food for adjacent Polders)

=== SCOTLAND ===

=== ZULU ===
